how it started

home

may 2023

the sheep

long story short: my parents have a few animals, including the very best sheep in the world (I cannot be convinced otherwise). his mother had some trouble giving birth, and they weren't sure if the baby would make it, but here we are, three years on. he's been raised with a lot of interaction so he's very friendly and as well-mannered as can be.

the wool

at the start of this year (2023) when visiting the parents, we'd discussed shearing this particular sheep, as he'd never experienced it before and none of us were sure how he'd handle it. luckily for all of us, he was an absolute gem about it and didn't panic in the slightest!

naturally, with it being his first shearing, there was a lot of very brittle wool, and plenty of debris throughout, but when given the opportunity to make something of the fibres, how could I say no?

the process

cleaning

this was by far the most arduous and time-consuming step of the process—many hours were spent sitting on the bathroom floor, separating the locks of wool from the bags of fleece, shaking out bits of hay and other debris, getting several alarmingly large splinters. the more usable fibres were placed into a laundry bag, ready for washing.

combing

this step I mostly do bit by bit as I spin each small section of the wool, using two dog brushes. I still have yet to determine the best way to prepare the fibres for the way I spin them, but hopefully it won't be too much of an ordeal.
