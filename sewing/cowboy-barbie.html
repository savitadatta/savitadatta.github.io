the cowboy barbie outfit

home

may 2023

in anticipation of the barbie movie appearing in cinemas in july, a few of us had decided to get dressed up for the opening night. naturally, this would involve wearing pink, but the thematic specifics were yet to be decided. now, I do have a pink outfit already, of course, but given the amount of time until the day itself, I'd also decided to do my best to create my own version of a specific look I'd seen to wear to the movie.

planning

components

this is a two-piece set, with flared pants and a singlet top with what looks like a combination collar-yoke. the top also has princess seams and two little pockets along the front seam lines, and the pants have stars on the flared part, along with two on the back and a decorative lace-up section at the waistband.

the fabric

I asked the girl at the fabric shop what she thought the outfit would need, and she suggested something like lycra, as it looked like it was made from some kind of smooth, stretchy fabric. however, I've never worked with anything like that before, and it was about $16/m, so I decided that the draft pattern-finding version/s would be with some other material, and if it works out well enough I might consider using the nice stuff.

first draft

the fabric

the draft fabric is some smooth, stretchy material that was $2/m at the fabric shop—a win, if you ask me.

the pants

the pieces

for the pattern pieces, I just traced some pants I already have, and cut out twice the amount I needed, in order to have a copy for future reference.

disaster and mayhem

in the blanket hoodie post I mentioned having some difficulties with the sewing machine jamming and throwing the spool all over the place—this project was where I finally asked for help, and was told to (a) use a straight stitch, and (b) find a needle better suited to handling stretchy fabric.

(the project is currently on hold until this happens.)
