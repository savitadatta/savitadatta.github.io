bacon and speculaas

home

may 2023

we had some leftover biscuits from eurovision, where we'd decided to bring snacks from the countries represented in the finals, so I figured it might be fun to make an ice cream from what was left.

but we simply couldn't just make a normal ice cream flavour, so…

the recipe for this is once again adapted from the semifreddo recipe mentioned in the last post, but adapted for the ingredients we had (no white sugar), and the fact that I didn't want to put brown sugar in the meringue. additionally, I was going from memory the whole time, so in all honesty I actually have no idea if it bears any resemblance to the original recipe.

ingredients

  • 6–9 speculaas biscuits
  • 4 pieces of short cut bacon, cut into small pieces
  • 3 eggs, separated
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 100–150ml heavy whipping cream

method

toppings

  1. cook the bacon until nicely browned and crispy (you could fry it but I baked it in the oven)
  2. crush (or blend) 4 of the biscuits into crumbs
  3. break the rest of the biscuits into chunks

the custard base

  1. combine the egg yolks with the sugar; whisk to combine
  2. continue whisking the egg mixture over a simmering pot of water until the sugar starts to dissolve
  3. remove from heat; continue whisking (it should get lighter in colour)
  4. repeat steps 2 and 3 if the vibes are right I guess

note: don't let the egg cook too much or it'll taste incredibly eggy!

the rest

  1. whip the egg whites to stiff peaks (opt. add cream of tartar and/or sugar if you have it)
  2. whip the cream to stiff peaks (soft in my case as I used a mini blender and didn't want to overdo it)
  3. add the whipped cream to the yolk mixture
  4. add the egg whites; fold together
  5. add bacon and biscuit crumbs
  6. layer biscuit chunks and the mixture in a container
  7. freeze until solid

the result

it's… pretty good? it's definitely a two-part experience, with the biscuit flavour at the forefront and the bacon only coming through at the end (which makes sense with its texture), but I rather like it.

changes for next time

  • use a normal hand mixer for the whipped cream instead of the tiny blender (not worth the effort and a lot gets left behind)
  • maybe save some bacon to crush smaller so it spreads more evenly and isn't always left to chew on at the end
