what would possess a person to make olive ice cream, you ask?

what an excellent question—my loved ones eagerly await an answer.

picture it: warm weather, jar of olives on the table... an idea struck and would not let go.

the first attempt

ingredients

  • 1 tbsp kalamata olives
  • 1 egg
  • heavy whipping cream
  • sugar
  • salt

method

knowing a first attempt was unlikely to be any good, I decided to adapt a semifreddo recipe which I've previously had some success with to take even less time and effort than it usually does, as well as keeping the batch small and splitting it in two to try both a sweet and savoury version. the process itself followed roughly the same general vibe as the recipe linked above:

  1. chop olives
  2. make 'custard' - in this case, whisking the egg yolk (I can't remember if I added sugar here or not)
  3. add olives
  4. whip cream to stiff peaks; add to olives/yolk
  5. whip egg whites to stiff peaks; add to the mixture
  6. split into two jars
  7. season to taste
  8. freeze for a few hours
  9. days later, remember having intended to add vanilla to the sweet one
  10. melt it down
  11. add vanilla
  12. refreeze

outcomes

well. it wasn't good.

in fairness, though, I did end up liking the sweet one, in some strange way, and I do intend to try again and hopefully do it better next time.
